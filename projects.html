Power BI

Customer Purchase Overview - SQL & Power BI

This dashboard visualizes key customer insights from a sales database, helping businesses track revenue performance, identify top customers, and understand regional purchasing trends over time.

My Role:
I used SQL Server to extract and transform the data, writing queries to answer over 20 business questions. After creating SQL views, I connected the data to Power BI and built interactive visuals — including KPI cards, customer distribution charts, and a sortable table of top customers. I also troubleshot challenges around slicer functionality by consolidating multiple SQL outputs into a single view.

Real-World Use:
This tool can help sales and marketing teams monitor customer engagement, analyze purchasing behavior by country, and track changes in revenue over time — all with slicers for Year, Month, and Country to support dynamic decision-making.

HR Analytics Dashboard

This dashboard explores key drivers of employee turnover, including job satisfaction, salary levels, and commute distance. It helps HR teams identify factors linked to attrition and improve staff retention strategies.

My Role:
I cleaned and transformed the data in Power BI, then used DAX to calculate key metrics such as attrition rates, average salaries by job role, and satisfaction breakdowns. I also applied a custom PowerPoint-designed background to improve layout consistency and visual storytelling.

Real-World Use:
This dashboard can support HR managers in uncovering high-risk turnover areas, evaluating the link between performance and pay, and identifying how commute distance affects workforce stability. It’s a practical tool for shaping better talent retention policies.

Cost Effectiveness Dashboard

This dashboard explores how hospital stay duration, discharge destination, and follow-up rates vary by age and gender. It provides insights into patient flow and resource use, which are key indicators of cost effectiveness in care.

My Role:
I used Power Query to clean and structure the data, and DAX to calculate average lengths of stay, follow-up percentages, and top diagnoses. The dashboard visualizes these patterns across gender and age brackets.

Real-World Use:
This tool can help hospital administrators identify high-cost diagnoses and adjust care pathways or discharge strategies to reduce unnecessary long stays and improve efficiency.

Excel

Superstore Sales Performance – Excel Dashboard

This dashboard analyzes product sales across Canadian provinces, uncovering trends in customer preferences and identifying top-performing regions and products.

My Role:
I used Excel to clean, analyze, and visualize the data — handling everything from raw data transformation to final dashboard design within Excel itself. The project highlights include province-level sales breakdowns, top-selling products, and a clear layout built entirely without external design tools. I replaced the original map with a more space-efficient bar chart and table to improve readability and layout.

Real-World Use:
This dashboard can support sales teams and regional managers in tracking product demand across locations, identifying best-selling items, and making informed decisions about inventory, promotions, and market expansion.
